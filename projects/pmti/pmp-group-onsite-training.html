What are the benefits of your project managers becoming PMP certified?

At the end of the day, projects either make or break a company which means the difference between millions of wasted dollars or millions of maximized revenue. Unfortunately, there's just no way around that fact for any company regardless of the industry.

The good news is, your company can safeguard itself from devastating loss and ensure its success with one simple fix: certify your employees with the PMI PMP Certification. Here's why:

  1. Your project managers will be trained on the PMI PMBOK Guide's best practices and ethics. They'll understand the framework of a successful project from initiating to close. What's more, is they won't just understand the roles, they'll be able to perform them too, which means they talk the talk and walk the walk.

  2. Trained PMs speak a universal language taught in PMI's PMBOK Guide which levels the playing field for team members over multiple projects. This will ensure your team acts as a cohesive unit and not like too many cooks in the kitchen. Gaps in communication are one of the leading causes of project failure.

  3. Since PMI's PMP certification is becoming the defacto standard for Government and Private organizations in every industry you can think of, more and more clients are making this a requirement.
